A Study on the Effect of Career Preferences on Mental Well-Being Among Students

Abstract

Career preferences are integral to students' development, shaping their academic pursuits, social interactions, and personal aspirations. While pursuing these preferences, students face immense pressure from intrinsic desires, societal expectations, and parental influences, potentially affecting their mental well-being.

This study explores the interplay between career preferences and mental health, identifying stressors such as academic pressure, societal norms, and the psychological toll of career uncertainty. Employing a mixed-methods approach, this research investigates the factors contributing to these challenges and proposes evidence-based interventions to support students’ mental well-being.

The findings reveal the urgent need for systematic support mechanisms, such as career counseling and mental health services, to foster resilience and satisfaction among students navigating career choices.
